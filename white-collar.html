01 — Overview

White collar defense is often about more than the charge, the allegation, or the agency involved. It is about managing risk at a moment when legal, reputational, and professional consequences may all be moving at once.


Blanchet handles this work with a clear understanding of what clients need early: a realistic assessment of exposure, a disciplined response, and lawyers who can manage the matter without adding unnecessary noise.

Some issues stay narrow. Others widen quickly. The point is to understand which is which, and to act accordingly.

02 — Problems

Problems Clients
Are Usually Facing

These matters often arrive with urgency, uncertainty, and more than one kind of pressure.

A subpoena, target letter, or government inquiry has arrived

The issue is now formal, time-sensitive, and likely to require immediate decisions about scope, response, and exposure.

An internal issue may have external consequences

What begins as a compliance concern, reporting issue, or personnel matter may quickly raise criminal or regulatory questions.

Civil and criminal risk may be developing at the same time

In some matters, the legal problem does not stay in one lane. Regulatory, civil, and reputational issues may all move together.

An executive or employee needs separate counsel

The company may have one set of interests. Individuals may have another. Those lines need to be handled carefully.

The facts are incomplete but decisions cannot wait

The record may still be developing, but preservation, communication, and response decisions often need to happen immediately.

The matter is sensitive beyond the legal issue itself

For some clients, confidentiality, business continuity, and reputational stability are just as important as the legal defense.

04 — Approach

How Blanchet
Approaches This Work

White collar matters tend to develop in unpredictable ways. Early decisions — about document preservation, internal communications, witness coordination, and engagement with regulators — often define the shape of everything that follows.


Blanchet's approach is direct and controlled. The firm assigns senior lawyers from the outset, keeps teams small, and focuses on understanding the full landscape of a client's exposure before recommending a course of action.

That means preparation for the possibility of trial — even when the goal is resolution. Credibility with prosecutors, regulators, and opposing counsel comes from being ready, not from saying you are.


Clients receive clear, honest assessments. Strategy is built around the facts as they are, not as anyone wishes they were. And communication stays consistent — with the client, with co-counsel, and with anyone on the other side.
