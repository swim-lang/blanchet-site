[ A NOTE FROM JOEL BLANCHET ]

Why we built Blanchet

Blanchet grew out of a simple view of how this work should be done.

Over time, many firms drift away from the thing clients are actually hiring them for. Teams get larger. Process gets heavier. The lawyers with the most judgment are asked to supervise the work rather than stay close to it.

We wanted to build something more direct.

A firm with real trial experience. A firm with broad disputes capability. A firm that could handle difficult litigation and investigations without losing discipline, pace, or accountability along the way.

That does not mean smaller in ambition. It means clearer in purpose.

We believe clients should know who is leading the matter. We believe technology should help move the work, not complicate it. We believe a strong firm should be able to handle demanding cases without turning every problem into a larger production.

Blanchet was built around those ideas. That is the standard we intend to keep.

[ TIMELINE ]

How Blanchet came together

The firm did not appear overnight. It took shape over years of litigation, trial work, client relationships, and lawyers finding their way back to a shared standard.

FOUNDATION

The standard was set early.

The foundation of the firm was shaped in demanding litigation environments where preparation, command of the record, and courtroom credibility were expected, not optional. That standard still informs how Blanchet approaches the work today.

Trial work came first.
RANGE

The work widened before the firm did.

Over time, the practice expanded across jurisdictions, forums, and subject matter. Commercial disputes, investigations, environmental issues, technical records, and other demanding matters all became part of the work.

Broad disputes capability.
BUFFALO

A different model proved itself.

Building a serious litigation practice from Buffalo reinforced an important point. Sophisticated work does not require a bloated structure. Clients responded to direct involvement, strong judgment, and teams that stayed close to the matter.

Closer to the client.
LAUNCH

Blanchet takes shape.

Blanchet launches as a focused litigation and investigations firm with a national practice and offices in Buffalo, New York City, and Chicago. The goal is not size for its own sake. The goal is quality, control, and staying close to the work.

Focused from the start.
NEXT

The ambition is discipline.

The future of the firm is not about becoming more sprawling. It is about becoming more trusted, more capable, and more effective while keeping the qualities that made the firm worth building in the first place.

Growth without drift.
[ POINT OF VIEW ]

A few views
we hold
pretty firmly.

Every firm has a way of talking about itself. More useful is a clear sense of what it actually believes.

01 / 06
TEAM

More lawyers does not always mean better lawyering.

Some matters need scale. Many do not. In our experience, larger teams often create more motion without creating better decisions. The right team is the one that understands the case and can move it forward with clarity.

TRIAL

Trial experience matters long before trial.

It changes how a case is assessed, how pressure points are identified, and how leverage is built. Trial experience is not just a credential at the end of the page. It changes the work from the beginning.

TECHNOLOGY

Technology should reduce drag.

Used well, technology improves review, coordination, and visibility. Used poorly, it becomes another layer of noise. We believe in using it where it helps and ignoring it where it does not.

CLIENTS

Clients are hiring judgment, not ceremony.

A polished process is not the same thing as good counsel. At some point, every difficult matter turns on judgment. That is one reason clients need direct access to the lawyers leading the work.

PRACTICE

A broad practice still needs a center.

Breadth is only useful if the firm knows what holds it together. For Blanchet, that center is disputes, investigations, and trial work. That is what keeps the practice coherent.

SERVICE

A good outside firm should feel close.

Clients should not feel distance as soon as a matter becomes active. They should know who is responsible, how the work is being handled, and where decisions are being made.

Blanchet was built to handle demanding work without losing the things that make demanding work manageable: clarity, accountability, and judgment.
